The app was brought live over the course of 48 hours as part of WirvsVirus Hackathon staged by the Federal German government. It aims to provide help to people living alone in lockdown by encouraging activities that help to maintain mental wellbeing and physical health.

Investigating problems

/All data and statistics are based on publicly available data at the time of publication./

Our team identified that the 2019‚Äì20 coronavirus pandemic and the resulting isolation as well as social distancing pose unprecedented challenge on people‚Äôs mental and emotional wellbeing. Especially those living in one person households: older people living alone, divorced people living alone, and single parents tend to face the most difficult times.

Through research, we identified a sample of problems that people living alone struggle with during COVID-19 lockdown:

  1. Managing moments of intense stress or anxiety, loneliness.
  2. Facing both physical and mental blocks that affect everyday lifestyle.
  3. Loosing connection to family and friends.
  4. Feeling out of balance with daily routine.

Knowing this information helped us frame design opportunity spaces that people can benefit from and stay more optimistic and engaged during tough times.

Objectives

Before starting to develop the project, we wanted to set a series of objectives that we wanted to achieve, to keep them in mind throughout the process:

  • Reduce the lack of social contact and isolation, that is, encourage people to get in ‚Äúcontact‚Äù with one another by means of technology, hand-written letters and sources of attention.
  • Focus on holistic health and social interaction by providing a place where people can be part of the community.
  • Keep the community physically and mentally fit, to promote their creativity, to let them discover new hobbies or to allow them to become artistically active, to learn new languages, etc.
  • Boost mood and mental wellbeing through engaging content.

Project Scope & MVP

Thanks to several project scope meetings it was agreed to deliver MVP in the form of a web-app as part of German Hackathon program against COVID-19. The product had to be built over the course of 48 hours with online collaboration only. Thanks to this approach we could deliver a working prototype which received support and appreciation and was selected in one of the 191 best in the hackathon among 1150 other submissions.

Research & AI (information architecture)

Before implementing the idea at Hackathon, we followed a series of research methodologies that helped us fully immerse ourselves in the subject matter and problem we were facing. Thanks to the use of this type of tools we can save time and money, since they allow us to validate or not ideas before they are implemented.

Competition

The first thing that was done was an analysis of direct and indirect competition in order to understand the environment in which we were entering, with the aim of seeking good and bad practices within the sector. Thanks to this method we can detect pain points in other products and patterns, which in the future will help us build our own product.

Survey Polls

Through surveys, we can obtain quantitative data about what users think about the subject we are dealing with.

A survey was launched using different digital communication channels such as Facebook, Twitter, WhatsApp and LinkedIn, resulting in 70 responses in total.

User Persona

Thanks to several conducted interviews with volunteers and data from the poll, we created archetypes, which represent the different groups of profiles of potential users of the product. These allow us to validate some decisions.

In this case we decided to create two, Molly , single woman who recently broke up with her boyfriend and now lives alone in a studio flat. On the other hand there is John , a father and grandpa who lives alone in the countryside while his son and family live in the city.

User stories & Functionality

Through user stories we were able to discover the tasks that the user needs our product to perform for him.

Below are some examples that came out during the exercise:

  • As a user when I am alone in my home, I want a tool that allows me to stay uplifted, and motivated throughout lonely moments.
    Functionality: A tool where you can participate in community challenges and fight against loneliness.

  • As a user I want to see the stats of the daily Germany challenge and how many I have succeeded.
    Functionality: Every day a nationwide task is shown, which we as a society have to master together, analogous to the crisis.

  • As a user I want to understand how the challenges contribute to my mental health.
    Functionality: Daily changing tips are displayed.

  • As a user I can select a category and receive a challenge for that topic.
    Functionality: Have a broad selection of categories to select the task from.

  • As a user I want to be able to make my challenges off-line.
    Functionality: Create tasks that actively make users send letters to relatives.

  • As a user I want to be able to submit my own challenge.
    Functionality: Be able to submit a new task to contribute to community.

  • As a user I want to share my results with others.
    Functionality: Successfully completed tasks can be shared by clicking on all common social media platforms..

  • As a user I want to receive some feedback from the tool.
    Functionality: Include reward system to motivate participants keep them engaged.

Colors

HomeAlone’s color theme uses one primary color for background (Light Violet) and two primary colors for text (Dark plum & HB graphite). Secondary colors add contract and impact to important information or call-to-action.

Onboarding

The illustrations in onboarding exemplify the key areas of the app, keeping the process simple and interesting for the user.

App personalization

User can go through the app personalization for more genuine user experience, alternatively this step can be skipped to be done later.

User profile & settings

A detailed user profile contains current challenge information and shows ongoing progress till the next milestone.

A useful gamifacition principle that gives the user a much-needed sense of progress and accomplishment in the early days until he finds the exercise itself to be intrinsically rewarding.

Access to settings is displayed in the upper right corner for quick accessibility.

Home page & Search

The app uses cards to show results, make selections and detailed search results. This enables quick and easy access to content.

Daily challenges

When a challenge is selected, informative description is presented below with a prominent CTA to participate. Further flow is enabling a user to complete the challenge and share the result by posting a photo with a small comment. This response will be visible to everyone who will select the same challenge.
